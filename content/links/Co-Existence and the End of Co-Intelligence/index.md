- The new book *Co-Existence* addresses working with AI systems whose capabilities are inconsistent, requiring humans to adapt rather than simply adopt them.
- Mollick employed AI strategically for fact-checking, editorial feedback, and web development while maintaining full authorship of drafts to preserve narrative voice and avoid long-form writing limitations.
- Content creators now face the emerging challenge of optimizing work for AI readers and agents, shifting from outdated prompt-injection tactics to transparent, test-driven design.
- Human-AI collaboration is an ongoing negotiation that must be continuously recalibrated as models improve and assume roles beyond assistance, such as critic or distribution gatekeeper.

The author argues that the AI landscape has fundamentally shifted from human-centered assistance to autonomous systems capable of outperforming people in targeted tasks, necessitating a new framework for professional collaboration. Rather than treating AI as a static tool, he frames it as a dynamic partner whose capabilities fluctuate, requiring creators and workers to constantly renegotiate boundaries, verify outputs, and adapt their workflows. This evolution matters because it transforms how work is produced, reviewed, and distributed, with AI increasingly acting as both collaborator and audience gatekeeper. Ultimately, long-term success depends on embracing this jagged frontier through deliberate, iterative negotiation rather than seeking a permanent technical solution."
